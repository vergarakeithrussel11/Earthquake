EARTHQUAKE HAZARDS

deepen your understanding about earthquakes, magnitudes, and intensities of an earthquake.

                 

EARTHQUAKE

An earthquake occurs when the ground suddenly shakes; it may be weak enough that you hardly notice it trembling, or it may be incredibly severe to the point that it affects and occasionally destroys buildings and homes. An earthquake happens when a rock beneath the earth's crust breaks quickly, releasing energy underground and creating motion and seismic waves that cause abrupt ground movement.

                 

MAGNITUDE

The earthquake's size is indicated by its magnitude. A seismometer can be used to measure the magnitude of an earthquake by recording and analyzing ground motion.

                 

INTENSITY

The intensity of an earthquake dictates how violently it shakes, as opposed to the magnitude, which primarily determines the size of the earthquake. Since some rocks and the majority of contemporary structures are made to resist earthquakes of higher intensity, the intensity may vary depending on where the earthquake is occurring.
